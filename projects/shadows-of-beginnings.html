Summary

Shadows of Beginnings is a top-down action RPG developed with NebulaInteractive. My primary focus on this project is level design, with an emphasis on player movement, encounter pacing, exploration, and creating spaces that support the game’s tone and gameplay goals.

This project is a strong example of my ability to work on a team, contribute to a shared vision, and design levels that support both gameplay and atmosphere.

Project Breakdown

  • Engine: Unreal Engine
  • Team Size: 4 developers
  • My Role: Level Design, blockouts, pacing, encounter support, Rogue class setup and design
  • Project Type: Educational team project
  • Tools Used: Unreal Engine, Git/GitHub, Blender

Preproduction

Before building the level spaces, I worked through planning and layout ideas to make sure the areas supported the player experience. This included rough top-down sketches, randomized travel logic prototype, and references for the overall mood of the game.

I also used planning visuals such as simple blockout sketches to think through how the player would move through each space and where the game should build pressure, slow down, or reward exploration.

Mechanics and Design

What did I do? I helped design and shape the level spaces so they supported the intended gameplay flow.

How did I do it? I used blockouts, top-down planning, and iteration in Unreal Engine to test spacing, movement paths, and pacing.

What was the purpose for the player? The goal was to make the world feel readable, engaging, and emotionally paced so the player could explore, fight, and progress without feeling lost or rushed.

Reflection

This project helped me improve my understanding of how level layout affects player pacing and tension. One of the biggest lessons was that a level is not just a space to move through, but a tool for guiding the player’s emotion and decisions.

Going forward, I want to keep improving how I document my design process and make my work easier for others to understand during showcase and review.
